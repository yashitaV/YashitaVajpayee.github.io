Hate Speech Detection

The project focuses on detecting hate speech and performing sentiment analysis on tweets using machine learning models. The dataset consists of 10,575 rows and three columns: id, label, and tweet. Data preprocessing techniques were used to clean and normalize the data, including lowercase conversion, URL and hashtag removal, punctuation elimination, and stop word removal. Visualization techniques were used to explore the data, including pie charts and word clouds for hate and non-hate tweets. The data was then divided into training and test sets, and several machine learning models were used, including logistic regression, random forest, Naive Bayes, SVM, and XGBoost. After comparing the accuracies of the models, XGBoost performed the best, but after hyperparameter tuning, Random forest performed better.
